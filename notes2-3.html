01 Proofs of Power & Product Rules

Proof of the Power Rule (Binomial Expansion)

This proof demonstrates why the power rule works for positive integer exponents. It relies on the limit definition of the derivative and the Binomial Theorem expansion [8]. 本推导展示了为什么幂法则适用于正整数指数。推导完全基于导数的极限定义以及二项式定理的展开式 [8]。
\[\begin{aligned} f'(x) &= \lim_{h\to0} \frac{(x+h)^n - x^n}{h} \quad \text{[Limit definition]} \\ &= \lim_{h\to0} \frac{\left(x^n + nx^{n-1}h + \frac{n(n-1)}{2}x^{n-2}h^2 + \cdots + h^n\right) - x^n}{h} \quad \text{[Binomial expansion]} \\ &= \lim_{h\to0} \frac{h\left(nx^{n-1} + \frac{n(n-1)}{2}x^{n-2}h + \cdots + h^{n-1}\right)}{h} \quad \text{[Cancel } x^n \text{ and factor } h\text{]} \\ &= \lim_{h\to0} \left(nx^{n-1} + \frac{n(n-1)}{2}x^{n-2}h + \cdots + h^{n-1}\right) \\ &= nx^{n-1} + 0 + \cdots + 0 = \mathbf{nx^{n-1}} \quad \text{[Evaluate limit as } h \to 0\text{]} \end{aligned} \quad [8]\]

Proof of the Product Rule (The Add/Subtract Trick)

To prove the product rule, we manipulate the limit definition algebraically. The crucial step is an algebraic trick: adding and subtracting the term \(f(x+h)g(x)\) in the numerator to factor by grouping [8, 9]. 在推导乘法求导法则时，核心代数技巧是在分子中同时加上并减去 \(f(x+h)g(x)\)，以便通过分组分解因式 [8, 9]。
\[\begin{aligned} \frac{d}{dx}[f(x)g(x)] &= \lim_{h\to0} \frac{f(x+h)g(x+h) - f(x)g(x)}{h} \\ &= \lim_{h\to0} \frac{f(x+h)g(x+h) \mathbf{- f(x+h)g(x) + f(x+h)g(x)} - f(x)g(x)}{h} \\ &= \lim_{h\to0} \left( f(x+h) \cdot \frac{g(x+h)-g(x)}{h} + g(x) \cdot \frac{f(x+h)-f(x)}{h} \right) \quad \text{[Group \& split]} \\ &= \left(\lim_{h\to0} f(x+h)\right) \cdot g'(x) + g(x) \cdot f'(x) \\ &= \mathbf{f(x)g'(x) + f'(x)g(x)} \quad \text{[Since } f \text{ is continuous, } \lim_{h\to0}f(x+h)=f(x)\text{]} \end{aligned} \quad [9]\]
02 Quotient Rule & Sine Derivative Proofs

Proof of the Quotient Rule (Fast Product Rule Method)

While the quotient rule can be proven using limits, it is much faster to prove using the Product Rule by setting up \(Q(x) = f(x)/g(x)\), rewriting it as multiplication, and differentiating [9]. 虽然商法则可以直接用极限定义来证明，但通过将商写为积的形式 \(Q(x)g(x) = f(x)\)，然后使用乘积法则求导，可以实现极为快速和精简的数学推导 [9]。
Let \(Q(x) = \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} \quad \implies \quad Q(x)g(x) = f(x)\) [9]

Differentiate both sides using the Product Rule [9]:
\(Q'(x)g(x) + Q(x)g'(x) = f'(x)\) [9]

Isolate \(Q'(x)\) and back-substitute \(Q(x)\) [9]:
\(Q'(x)g(x) = f'(x) - Q(x)g'(x)\) [9]
\(Q'(x) = \frac{f'(x) - \left(\frac{f(x)}{g(x)}\right)g'(x)}{g(x)}\) [9]

Multiply numerator and denominator by \(g(x)\) [9]:
\[Q'(x) = \mathbf{\frac{f'(x)g(x) - f(x)g'(x)}{[g(x)]^2}}\]

Proof of the Sine Derivative (Limits & Trig Identities)

Trigonometric proofs rely heavily on the angle addition identity \(\sin(A+B)\) and two special trigonometric limits that act as prerequisites [10]. 正弦函数导数的推导需要使用和角公式 \(\sin(A+B)\) 展开，并依赖于两个重要的三角学极限先决条件 [10]。
\[\begin{aligned} \frac{d}{dx}[\sin x] &= \lim_{h\to0} \frac{\sin(x+h) - \sin x}{h} \\ &= \lim_{h\to0} \frac{(\sin x \cos h + \cos x \sin h) - \sin x}{h} \quad \text{[Angle addition identity]} \\ &= \lim_{h\to0} \frac{\sin x(\cos h - 1) + \cos x \sin h}{h} \quad \text{[Group terms]} \\ &= \sin x \cdot \left(\lim_{h\to0} \frac{\cos h - 1}{h}\right) + \cos x \cdot \left(\lim_{h\to0} \frac{\sin h}{h}\right) \quad \text{[Split limits]} \\ &= (\sin x)(0) + (\cos x)(1) = \mathbf{\cos x} \quad \text{[Substitute special limits]} \end{aligned} \quad [10]\]

04 Proof-Based Worked Examples

Example 01: Limit Proof of Cosine Derivative

Use the limit definition of the derivative and trig identities to prove that \(\frac{d}{dx}[\cos x] = -\sin x\). 套用导数的极限定义与三角恒等式，证明余弦函数的导数为 \(-\sin x\)。
SYSTEM LOG: Trigonometric Proof Protocol Initialized
Angle Addition: \(\cos(x+h) = \cos x \cos h - \sin x \sin h\). 余弦的和角公式：\(\cos(x+h) = \cos x \cos h - \sin x \sin h\)。
\[\begin{aligned} \frac{d}{dx}[\cos x] &= \lim_{h\to0} \frac{\cos(x+h) - \cos x}{h} \\ &= \lim_{h\to0} \frac{(\cos x \cos h - \sin x \sin h) - \cos x}{h} \\ &= \lim_{h\to0} \frac{\cos x(\cos h - 1) - \sin x \sin h}{h} \quad \text{[Group terms]} \\ &= \cos x \cdot \left(\lim_{h\to0} \frac{\cos h - 1}{h}\right) - \sin x \cdot \left(\lim_{h\to0} \frac{\sin h}{h}\right) \quad \text{[Split limits]} \\ &= (\cos x)(0) - (\sin x)(1) = \mathbf{-\sin x} \quad \text{[Substitute special limit values]} \end{aligned}\]

Example 02: Secant Proof via Quotient Rule

Use the Quotient Rule to prove that \(\frac{d}{dx}[\sec x] = \sec x \tan x\), rewriting \(\sec x\) as \(\frac{1}{\cos x}\). 利用商法则推导割线函数 \(\sec x\) 的导数为 \(\sec x \tan x\)（提示：将 \(\sec x\) 重写为 \(\frac{1}{\cos x}\)）。
SYSTEM LOG: Reciprocal Derivative Algorithm
Numerator \(f(x) = 1 \implies f'(x) = 0\), Denominator \(g(x) = \cos x \implies g'(x) = -\sin x\). 设分子 \(f(x) = 1 \implies f'(x) = 0\)，分母 \(g(x) = \cos x \implies g'(x) = -\sin x\)。
\[\begin{aligned} \frac{d}{dx}[\sec x] &= \frac{d}{dx}\left[\frac{1}{\cos x}\right] \\ &= \frac{(0)(\cos x) - (1)(-\sin x)}{\cos^2 x} \quad \text{[Apply Quotient Rule: } \frac{f'g - fg'}{g^2}\text{]} \\ &= \frac{\sin x}{\cos^2 x} \\ &= \frac{1}{\cos x} \cdot \frac{\sin x}{\cos x} \quad \text{[Split fraction]} \\ &= \mathbf{\sec x \tan x} \quad \text{[Apply trigonometric identities]} \end{aligned}\]
