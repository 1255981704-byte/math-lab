01 The Power Rule & Linearity
The Power Rule is the fundamental shortcut for differentiating power functions, avoiding the long limit definition. Linearity rules allow us to differentiate terms one by one and pull constants out to the front [4]. 幂法则是求导幂函数的基础快捷法则，可以避免繁琐的极限极限定义。线性求导法则则支持我们对多项式逐项进行求导，并允许将常数系数提取至导数符号外部 [4]。
\[\text{Power Rule: } \frac{d}{dx}[x^n] = n x^{n-1} \quad (\text{for any real } n)\] \[\text{Linearity: } \frac{d}{dx}[c \cdot f(x)] = c \cdot f'(x) \quad \text{and} \quad \frac{d}{dx}[f(x) \pm g(x)] = f'(x) \pm g'(x)\]
Strategy: Rewriting is Key 求导策略：重写结构是关键
  • >> Rewrite rational denominators as negative exponents: \(\frac{5}{x^3} \to 5x^{-3}\) [4]
  • >> Convert radicals to fractional powers: \(\sqrt[4]{x} \to x^{1/4}\) [4]
  • >> Apply Power Rule, then simplify back to root/positive exponent formats. [4]
02 Multiplication & Division Rules
When two separate functions containing \(x\) are multiplied or divided, we cannot simply multiply or divide their derivatives. We must apply specific structural formulas [4]. 当两个含有自变量 \(x\) 的独立函数相乘或相除时，绝对不能直接将它们的导数进行乘除。我们必须严格套用以下结构公式 [4]。
\[\text{The Product Rule: } \frac{d}{dx}[f(x)g(x)] = f'(x)g(x) + f(x)g'(x) \quad \text{("1st' }\cdot \text{ 2nd} + \text{ 1st} \cdot \text{ 2nd'")} \quad [5]\] \[\text{The Quotient Rule: } \frac{d}{dx}\left[\frac{f(x)}{g(x)}\right] = \frac{f'(x)g(x) - f(x)g'(x)}{[g(x)]^2} \quad \text{("}\frac{\text{Low d-High} - \text{High d-Low}}{\text{Low-Square}}\text{")} \quad [5]\]
Protocol: When to AVOID Quotient Rule 规避策略：何时应避免商法则
If the denominator is a single term (monomial), divide each term first to bypass the complicated quotient algorithm [5]. 如果分母是单项式，可先对分子各项进行除法化简，化为多项式后再使用简单的幂求导法则 [5]。
\[y = \frac{x^4 - 2x^2}{x} \quad \implies \quad y = x^3 - 2x \quad \implies \quad y' = 3x^2 - 2 \quad [5]\]
03 Transcendental & Trig Derivatives
Transcendental functions (Trigonometric, Exponential, Logarithmic) operate differently than algebraic powers. Memorizing exact derivative relationships and behaviors is essential [6]. 超越函数（三角函数、指数与对数函数）的求导特性与常规的代数幂函数明显不同。熟记这些解析关系和斜率性质是微积分的核心考点 [6]。
TRIG DERIVATIVE PHASING (\(\sin x \to \cos x\))
Slope of \(\sin x\) (White) exactly maps to height of \(\cos x\) (Cyan) [6]. \(\sin x\)（白色）的切线斜率变化完美重合于 \(\cos x\)（青色）的函数高度 [6]。
NATURAL EXPONENTIAL INVARIANCE (\(e^x \to e^x\))
The slope (Orange tangent) at any point equals the y-value of \(e^x\) [6]. 在 \(e^x\) 曲线上任何一点处的切线斜率（橙色）均严格等于该点的纵坐标值 [6]。

Base \(e\) derivatives remain natively simple. General bases require a scaling factor of \(\ln a\) [6]. 以自然底数 \(e\) 为底的导数形式最简；一般底数（如 \(a^x\)）求导时必须带上修正项 \(\ln a\) [6]。

05 Worked Examples

Example 01: Product Rule & Algebra

Find the derivative of \(y = x^3 \cdot \sin x\), and factor the final answer [5]. 计算 \(y = x^3 \cdot \sin x\) 的导数，并对最终答案提取公因式进行化简 [5]。
SYSTEM LOG: Product Rule Applied [5]
Let \(f(x) = x^3\) and \(g(x) = \sin x\). Then, \(f'(x) = 3x^2\) and \(g'(x) = \cos x\). 令分项函数：\(f(x)=x^3\)，\(g(x)=\sin x\)。对应的分项导数为：\(f'(x)=3x^2\)，\(g'(x)=\cos x\)。
\[y' = \frac{d}{dx}[x^3] \cdot \sin x + x^3 \cdot \frac{d}{dx}[\sin x] \quad \text{[Apply Product Rule structural formula]} \quad [5]\] \[y' = (3x^2)(\sin x) + (x^3)(\cos x) \quad \text{[Differentiate terms individually]} \quad [5]\] \[y' = \mathbf{x^2(3\sin x + x\cos x)} \quad \text{[Factor out the greatest common factor } x^2\text{]} \quad [5]\]

Example 02: Deriving Tangent via Quotient Rule

Prove that \(\frac{d}{dx}[\tan x] = \sec^2 x\) by using the Quotient Rule on \(\frac{\sin x}{\cos x}\) [7]. 通过在 \(\frac{\sin x}{\cos x}\) 上套用商法则，证明正切函数的导数为 \(\sec^2 x\) [7]。
SYSTEM LOG: Analytical Trigonometric Proof [7]
Let top \(u = \sin x \implies u' = \cos x\), and bottom \(v = \cos x \implies v' = -\sin x\). 令分子 \(u=\sin x \implies u'=\cos x\)，分母 \(v=\cos x \implies v'=-\sin x\)。
\[y' = \frac{\frac{d}{dx}(\sin x) \cdot \cos x - \sin x \cdot \frac{d}{dx}(\cos x)}{(\cos x)^2} \quad \text{[Apply Quotient Rule: } \frac{u'v - uv'}{v^2}\text{]} \quad [7]\] \[y' = \frac{(\cos x)(\cos x) - (\sin x)(-\sin x)}{\cos^2 x} \quad \text{[Differentiate]} \quad [7]\] \[y' = \frac{\cos^2 x + \sin^2 x}{\cos^2 x} \quad \text{[Simplify numerator]} \quad [7]\] \[y' = \frac{1}{\cos^2 x} \quad \text{[Apply Pythagorean Identity: } \sin^2 x + \cos^2 x = 1\text{]} \quad [7]\] \[y' = \mathbf{\sec^2 x} \quad \text{[Apply Reciprocal Identity]} \quad [7]\]
